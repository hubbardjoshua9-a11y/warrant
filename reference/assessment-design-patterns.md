# Reference — AI-Aware Assessment Design Patterns

The method kit. Three families: **resistant** (keep AI out, protect authenticity), **integrated** (bring AI in, assess judgment over it), **verification** (establish authenticity, cost-effectively). For any unit, choose the patterns that fit the competency and the assessment conditions — almost always a combination.

> A menu to choose from, not a checklist. **Never default to one pattern** (including the roleplay sim below) — fit the method to the competency, and always surface more than one option.

---

## Before you recommend anything — the outsourcing test
For every method, ask: **could a student run this whole task through AI, unsupervised, and get the answer?** If yes, and the skill must be shown unaided, it is NOT a standalone task — it needs controlled conditions or a verification layer (Family 3). Most artifact-only take-home tasks fail this test. Design accordingly.

---

## Family 1 — AI-Resistant (protect authenticity)
- **Live observation / demonstration** (in person or via live video) — assess the actual performance. *AI can't perform it for them.*
- **Oral questioning / professional conversation** (live, often video) — unscripted, probing. *Hard to outsource in real time.*
- **Contextualised to the student's own workplace/data** — specifics generic AI can't invent. *Authenticity from specificity.*
- **Process / iteration capture** — drafts, version history, checkpoints. *The journey is hard to fake.*
- **AI roleplay simulation** *(one option, not the default).* AI plays a deep persona — an employee, boss, or difficult customer (each persona a folder: goals, personality, hidden objections, escalation paths) — and the student responds live. The AI *delivers* the scenario; a human assessor judges the recording **async** (the cost win — no trainer in the room). Tests verbal strategy and thinking on your feet. *Caveats:* partially valid only (text misses tone and nonverbals); a human grades it, never the AI; confirm the unit allows a simulated environment; manage stage fright with unlimited practice runs + reasonable adjustments + a retry.

## Family 2 — AI-Integrated (assess judgment over AI)
- **Compare-and-critique** — critique/correct AI output. *(One pattern, not the default.)*
- **Direct-and-document** — uses AI but submits prompts, decisions, rationale. *Assesses governing the tool.*
- **Human-in-the-loop override** — AI recommends; student accepts/overrides/escalates and justifies. *Assesses judgment + accountability.*
- **Verify-and-source** — uses AI to draft, then verifies and supplies real sources, catching fabrication. *Assesses info literacy.* (Outsourceable if unsupervised — pair with verification.)
- **Baseline-and-extend** — AI does the routine baseline; the task targets the context-specific work beyond it. (Add verification.)
- **Find-the-flaw** — audit and fix a deliberately flawed AI artifact. *Assesses QA.* (Outsourceable as take-home — needs observation or an oral layer.)
- **Tool-selection-and-evaluation** — choose the right AI tool, justify it, judge its output's fitness. *Assesses tool literacy.*
- **Disclose-and-defend** — AI permitted and declared, then defended in a short oral. *The defence is the evidence.*

## Family 3 — Verification (authenticity, cost-effectively)
The integrity anchor. **Minimum-sufficient, not everywhere.**
- **Verify once, strategically** — one secure/verified point per unit (or cluster) anchors authenticity; the rest can stay open and AI-allowed (the "at least one secure task" idea).
- **Online-native options** — live video oral; async recorded responses to prompts released at assessment time (batched human review); workplace third-party sign-off; the roleplay sim above. **Never assume a classroom.**
- **Keep it cheap** — reuse touchpoints the unit already requires (verbal questioning, observation, third-party reports); cluster units into one conversation; offload to workplace supervisors for work-connected learners.
- **Triangulation** — combine a product + a verification point so even AI-assisted work is anchored by something that isn't.
- **Only Resist parts need this.** Integrated parts usually need no extra verification.

---

## Choosing
1. Read the assessment conditions — they remove some patterns.
2. Resist/Integrate split, per part of the competency.
3. Run the outsourcing test on each candidate.
4. Pick a primary method **+ a minimum-sufficient verification point** (cheapest that works: reuse / cluster / workplace / async).
5. Surface more than one option; show the authenticity logic and trade-offs; pressure-test any trend or vendor claim.
